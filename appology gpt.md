# Apology Context

The original project was supposed to be a love letter and a tribute to Dounia.

Something changed.

During a fight, we were both angry, cursing, and saying hurtful things. In that moment, I said something cruel that I deeply regret: I told her that she was not worthy of marriage, halal, and respect.

That was wrong.

Even if I was angry, frustrated, and hurt, there is no excuse for saying something like that. It was degrading, unfair, and it likely hit one of the deepest wounds she carries. Regardless of what the argument was about, she did not deserve to hear that from me.

The truth is that I have been wanting to marry her for more than a year, and part of my frustration came from feeling helpless and worried. She keeps delaying marriage, and I also worry about her daily cab rides to work in Casablanca because I see that as unsafe and I wish I could be the one picking her up as her husband. But those feelings do not justify what I said.

What I said does not reflect what I truly believe about her.

She is worthy of respect.
She is worthy of dignity.
She is worthy of marriage.
She is worthy of being treated gently.

What happened here is that I failed her in a painful moment. I let anger speak in a way that attacked her worth instead of addressing the actual issue. That is on me.

If this project continues, it cannot just be a celebration anymore. It also has to come from truth, remorse, and accountability. Not in a way that manipulates forgiveness, but in a way that honestly recognizes the damage done.

The repair has to start with full ownership:

> I said something cruel and degrading. It was false, and you did not deserve it. My anger is not an excuse. I understand why that would hurt you deeply, especially coming from me. I am sorry.

This context matters because the story is no longer only about showing her that she is seen and loved. It is also about facing the fact that I became the source of pain in a moment when I should have protected her heart.
