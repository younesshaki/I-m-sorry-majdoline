## The Emotional Shape

A story has a shape — not just a beginning/middle/end, but a feeling that builds.

| Stage | Emotional state | What the audience feels |
|-------|----------------|------------------------|
| Opening | Warmth, innocence, the world before it broke | Safety — we are shown what there was to lose |
| Rising | Childhood joy — bikes, sports, both parents working, she is free | A girl fully alive, before she learns to protect herself |
| Break / rupture | The door. Father says "wait for me." Never comes back. Mother taken. Age 9. | The ground disappearing. Grief for a child who had no language for what just happened. |
| Long middle | Survival mode — Mommy Meriem, carrying Ayoub, then university, dancing, becoming someone formidable | Awe at what she built from nothing. And the quiet sadness of watching someone grow strong for the wrong reasons. |
| Turning point | Youness. Someone who stayed. Someone who came back after every break. Someone who sees her. | The terrifying feeling of a door that could open. |
| Arrival | 25 years old. Hijab. Still tired. Still carrying. But no longer alone. | Not rescue — recognition. She was never broken. She was becoming. |
