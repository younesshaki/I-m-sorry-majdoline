# Sorry — Chapter Content

This file is the creative and emotional blueprint for the "Sorry" experience.
It is the source of truth for what Dounia will see, hear, and feel when she opens this.

---

## The Wound

During a fight, both of us mad and cursing, I told her she is not worthy of marriage, halal, and respect.

I should have never said that.

The truth is the opposite — I have been trying to marry her for more than a year. I want to pick her up from work as her husband because taking cabs alone in Casablanca every day terrifies me. The anger came from love and fear, not from what I said.

But that doesn't erase it. She heard it. And this is my answer to that.

---

## What This Chapter Is

Not a grand cinematic story. Not a celebration. Just one thing:

**I see you. I'm sorry. And here is proof that I mean it.**

The experience should feel intimate, quiet, and heavy — like a letter read alone at night.
It should not feel like a spectacle. It should feel like truth.

---

## Emotional Arc (Scene Sequence)

### Scene 1 — The Weight of What Was Said
*Tone: Dark. Quiet. Honest.*

The opening lands in darkness. Silence or near-silence.
A single line appears — the exact thing I said to her.
Not softened. Not explained. Just placed there so she knows I remember exactly what it was.

Then it fades.

What comes next is not a defense. It is context offered with humility.
A voiceover (my voice) explains what was underneath the anger:
the year of trying to marry her, the cabs, the helplessness of watching someone you love take risks you can't protect her from yet.

But the voiceover makes clear: *none of that makes it okay.*

---

### Scene 2 — Who She Actually Is
*Tone: Warm. Slow. Reverent.*

This is the counter to what I said.

The scene becomes something living — particles, light, soft movement.
One by one, truths about her appear. Not compliments. Observations. Things only I would notice.

- She is the most disciplined person I have ever known.
- She has been carrying her entire family on her back for four years without asking anyone for help.
- She became a hijabi at 25, a step she thought about for years, quietly, on her own terms.
- She is a survivor who never calls herself one.
- She is terrified, and she doesn't show it, and I see it anyway.
- She is deeply sensitive behind the nonchalance, and I love both versions.

The camera (or visual) slowly moves forward as these truths accumulate.
Not triumphant — tender.

---

### Scene 3 — The Promise
*Tone: Still. Grounded. Final.*

No performance here. No music swell.

My voice again. Directly to her.

What I promise:
- That I will never say something like that again.
- That I know what survival mode feels like — I have lived it — and I will not let her face her worst fears alone.
- That the reason I have been pushing for marriage is not impatience. It is that I want to be there. Officially. Fully. As her husband.
- That she deserves halal, respect, and marriage more than anyone I know.
- That I am not going anywhere.

The scene ends. No title card. No credits. Just her name.

**Dounia.**

Or maybe her other name.

**Majdoline.**

---

## Visual Direction Notes

- No bright colors in the early scenes. Deep blacks, dark blues, maybe ember-warm light emerging in scene 2.
- No 3D character models needed initially — abstract particles, light fields, depth-of-field effects.
- Scene 3 could be the simplest visually — almost still — because the words carry it.
- The title card video (before Play) should feel like her world: Casablanca, evening light, something familiar.

---

## Music Notes

- Scene 1: minimal or none. Let silence do the work.
- Scene 2: soft ambient — something that builds slowly without being cinematic or overly emotional.
- Scene 3: fade out to near-silence by the end. The last moment should be quiet.
- Reference mood: Nils Frahm, Ólafur Arnalds, or something from the Arabic/French world that connects to her.

---

## What She Should Feel at the End

Not entertained. Not impressed.

Seen.

She should close this feeling like someone finally held up a mirror and said:
*This is who you are. And I'm not going anywhere.*
